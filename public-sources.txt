## Best matches for your needs

- Race cards, runners, horse, trainer, jockey, track info: Racing Queensland, Equibase, The Racing API, and Betfair are the best matches because they expose entry-level meeting and race data. [news.ycombinator](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=7779117)
- Form guides and historical performance: Racing Queensland, Equibase/Stats Central, Racing and Sports, and The Racing API are the most relevant. [racingandsports.com](https://www.racingandsports.com.au/thoroughbred/trainer)
- Steward reports / integrity reports: Racing Queensland and British Horseracing Authority are the clearest public sources I found. [racingqueensland.com](https://www.racingqueensland.com.au/about/reports-and-data/open-data)
- Stable locations: public trainer/stable profile pages exist on Racing and Sports and some trainer directories, but true stable-location data is usually scattered and not consistently exposed as structured open data. [horseracing.com](https://horseracing.com.au/trainer/)
- Speed map / sectional / live position data: this is usually commercial rather than open, with Total Performance Data and similar vendors providing live tracking and race metrics. [totalperformancedata](https://www.totalperformancedata.com)

## What to expect

Public “open URL” access usually means one of three things: a public website you can scrape, an open-data portal, or an API that requires registration but is publicly documented. For the exact fields you listed, Racing Queensland is the most explicit open-data source because it mentions nominations, acceptances, weights, fields, form, gear changes, results, shoe reports, scratchings, jockey alterations, and steward reports. For deeper historical/statistical databases, Equibase and The Racing API are stronger, but they are more API/product-driven than fully open government-style data portals.
